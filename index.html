History of the font

Forma is a neo-grotesque typeface originally designed in 1968 by Aldo Novarese and a team at the Italian type foundry Nebiolo. It was intended as Italy’s answer to Helvetica, featuring a more refined and sophisticated approach to the Swiss-style sans serif. However, it never achieved the same widespread popularity and eventually faded into obscurity. In 2016, type designer David Jonathan Ross revived and expanded Forma, releasing it as Forma DJR. His reinterpretation preserved the crisp elegance of the original while adapting it for contemporary digital typography. Ross’s version offers a wider range of weights, improved spacing, and additional stylistic refinements, making it a versatile choice for modern design while honoring its mid-century Italian roots.
